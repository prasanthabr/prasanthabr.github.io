Green paddy fields laced with river lets, water bodies, lagoons and lakes, tall coconut palms that wave its leaves in cool breeze, pleasant sun shine during day time and cool moon light during night and rich array of flora and fauna make Kuttanadu the beauty spot of Keralam. Like a traveler who cannot afford to leave Egypt without seeing the Pyramids, or who cannot miss to see the Niagara Falls before leaving Canada, a tourist coming to Kerala cannot complete the tour with satisfaction without a visit to Kuttanadu. 


A short trip through the lakes and lagoons and a night stay in one of the house boats of Kuttanadu enjoying the tasty local cuisine will remain afresh for long in the minds of any tourist who gets at least a single occasion to enjoy this. Most of the house boats serve local delicacies like ‘Kappayum meen vevichathum’ (Tapioca and a special fish curry using kudampuli- an exclusive spice of Kuttanadu) and Karimeen pollichathu (A special fry of Pearlspot packed in banana leaves giving the preparation a rich aroma that arouses taste buds). There are House boats with more than one bed rooms that can accommodate quite a number of families. Most of the House boats restrict accommodation inside the boat only to families who know each other; they even keep the second or third bed room vacant if you are alone. Different types of House Boats are available to suit the purse of the tourist. Some may be fitted with AC and some others will be Non AC. Some are really palatial.  Those who love to enjoy the Monsoons may visit Kuttanadu during June, July & August. Others can come during the remaining months of the year. Advance booking is advisable during September to February when there will be heavy rush of tourists. The rate per day per boat may go above Rs.10000/- during these months depending upon the amenities they are providing. House boats are available at various places in Kuttanadu, especially in Alleppey and Pallathuruthy (a place on the eco driveway marked in the map)


House Boats of Kuttanadu took the present form only during the last few decades. People of Kuttanadu had been using big wooden boats (called Pathemari or Kevu vallam) for moving cargo (mainly paddy, coconut, copra etc) through lakes and rivers. These boats were usually moved manually using bamboo poles and ores. Most of these boats had also masts tied with mats made of strong dry fibrous leaves of a local plant known as kaitha. This was for making use to wind energy for moving the boats while travelling through vast stretches of lakes. Many of these pathemaris had covered roof made of coconut leaves and bamboo to protect the cargo from rain. The men travelling in these boats used to cook their own food inside the boats itself and used to sleep on the deck during night. The songs sung and the sounds made by these men transformed Kuttanadan life lively during those evenings. It was really a fascinating site seeing groups of pathemaris with mats raised, moving in a row through lakes during those days.


As the poet says, “That time is past……”


At the advent of modern transporting systems and depletion of waterways, those pathemaris were fast moving out of site for a period. However, people of Kuttanadu, converted their old pathemaries to suit to the changing times. They now use it as house boats for carriying tourists through the lagoons and lakes. The modern house boats of Kuttanadu really bear witness to the adaptability, creativity and resilience of an imaginative people.
