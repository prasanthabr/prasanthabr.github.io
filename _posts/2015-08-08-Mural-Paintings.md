From time immemorial, Mural Paintings and inscriptions on walls had been a major medium of public communication, since messages and ideas could be communicable universally through this medium. Great cycles of religious stories were spread across the walls of ecclesiastical structures almost as Christianity became state religion in the West. In Kerala, though Mural paintings are generally associated with Hindu temples and temple architecture, instances of the use of this medium in Christian churches are seen in some of the ancient Christian churches too. Such Christian murals attract constant attention of art lovers visiting the state. 
 
The walls of St. Mary’s Church Kottayam, usually known as Kottayam Cheriyapalli, are adorned with beautiful murals on bibical and non bibical thems, made in oriental and western styles. The mural paintings on the walls of the church, as old as the church itself, depict various Christian stories. Cheriyapalli has some fairly large and comparatively fine murals. These paintings include the last Supper, Judas accepting the silver for his betrayal, Jesus’ disciples waiting for him in the garden of Gethsemane, Jesus being whipped and dragged publicly, His trial, Pontius Pilate washing his hands after condemning Christ to the cross, the Crucifixion, the Virgin Mother with the body of Christ on her lap and Christ’s ascension. But here, one cannot help noticing the colour of Christ’s robe, which is ochre rather than white. Ochre in Hindu concept is a color related to mysticism and spirituality.
 
Another ancient church where mural paintings are present is the Paliakara church near Thiruvalla.  The murals on the eastern wall of the altar of this church attract many tourists and continue to be the subject of study for numerous students and teachers of History. These murals which have been reasonably well preserved are of very high quality and are painted using natural vegetable dyes with natural components.
 
These murals depict the major events in the life of Lord Jesus Christ on earth. Below these murals there is a huge picture of St.George the patron Saint of the Church. To either side of this picture of St George there is a picture of the twelve apostles of Lord Jesus Christ. The names of the Apostles are seen written in Syriac below this painting.
 
The construction of the roof of the Church is different and unique. On either side of the big wooden girders supporting the roof, one can see small figures of tigers and elephants. During the process of renovation of the Church in 2005, inscriptions were found on the wall above the Baptismal Font, and this can also be seen at different places on the walls of the Church. The “winged” facade of the church and the figures carved on the facade represent a unique style of Church architecture.
