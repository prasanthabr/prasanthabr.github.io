Thoughts on the hows of note taking  

- Notes have to be generative  
This has been practically very difficult for me, the idea being listen to the
conversation, comprehend it, and generate the ideas in your own words. What is
challenging is often its hard for me to understand what is being said. The
general consensus is this is a lack of paying attention and a lack of depth on
previous topics. Will write thoughts on how to address that.  

- Such generative notes can be linked to key ideas and words  
This requires a better overall understanding on what is being spoken about.
The notes above can pull out key themes from them.  

- Don't do verbatim  
Its easier, but its a waste. As a consultant, you are not a scribe.
Pay attention, comprehend what is said and make notes.  

- Summarize  
Just on conclusion summarize the entire thing so as to have a tl;dr

- Actions and Next Steps  
Always capture what should happen as the consequence of this meeting or
conversation.

- Document and Link Notes  
These notes in isolation do provide value _maybe_, but it is more valuable when
linked to other ideas and thoughts.

- Practice this  
Getting better at this needs more practice.

- Preloading success  
Prepare for the meeting and understand what to expect.  
Note down what outcomes are important for you from the meeting.  
I have tried creating headings, but have struggled - probably coz of the
comprehension issues - better may be to keep a list of key topics to check off
in a list.

- Potential Format  
Section for key topics expected.
Notes in prose  
Key words as tag section
Summary
Actions
