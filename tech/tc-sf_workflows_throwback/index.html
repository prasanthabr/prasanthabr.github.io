So why would you use Workflows at all now?!

Posted on 2018, Jul 03 by Prasanth Abraham 3 mins read

tl;dr Workflows are quirky in their execution. Custom validations are not triggered on a workflow update, while a process builder triggers custom validations.

Key Challenge: reminding oneself of things you know as you become more familiar with newer tools and their behaviour. Key Note: Desiging based on quirks should be considered an anti-pattern.

Background

Salesforce with its emphasis on building low code tools, had been pampering admins with tools allow for more sophosticaed solutions without the use of Developer skills or time. Admins can now leverage ever more powerful tools such as (though not without its failings - more on it later) flows (and process builders) that allow creation of solutions that were difficult to achieve in a configuration only environment.

Shiny new toys like this, would perhaps lead us to question the relevance of older, mature tools. I had an encounter of a similar nature last week. In a maze of validation rules and process automation, the use case demanded the update of a flag bypassing the validation rules. The idea being validation rules are meant for user alone, something akin to making a field requried on the page layout as opposed to the field defintion.

Process Builder and Lightning Flows

Per the order of execution, the validation rules kick in early, with the process builders and flows kicking in further down the line after the after trigger are called.

Unfortunately, when working with flows and validation rules, what I have seen is, a validation error does not seem to stop the cycle and process proceeds to the flow. The flow throws an error indicating that the validation has failed.

Since we dont get the most user friendly of errors here, end users suspect (even accuse) the flow to be the reason for any failures since the error is triggered from the flow.

Validation Error Catch all in Flows

The work around that I came up with was to drag a fault line from the DML element to email trigger or a screen so that the error is caught at the flow elegantly (image below). What this does is that the flow, fails silently - throws the error fault line action; the transactions continues and the validation error is shown as expected on the screen.

The side effect

This work around works well, when the designer is happy with the entire transaction failing and rolling back. At times though, it leads to a stalemate, resulting is validation errors stopping any progress to the record, leading to errors leading to errors.

The Quirk

While the flow gets the brunt of the validation error, his older cousin, the workflow is able to seemingly operate without triggering the validation rule. Why is that?!

The answer to this question would be on the order of execution which indicates

Executes workflow rules.

If there are workflow field updates, updates the record again.

If the record was updated with workflow field updates, fires before update triggers and after update triggers one more time (and only one more time), in addition to standard validations. Custom validation rules, duplicate rules, and escalation rules are not run again.

A client of mine (hattip - Sanchit), pointed this out to me the other day and the only reasonable explanation I could come up with for not knowing (i would like to think, remembering) this, is due to the relative lack of hands on on older tools, when the shiny new toy comes along!

Conculsion

So a close and careful reading of the features of Salesforce.com element would perhaps allow us to weave a solution that would fit the requirements. However, as a designer, I would strive to setup a solution that operates consistently and provides an easier solution for the support and admin users who follow to groom and maintain the solution.
